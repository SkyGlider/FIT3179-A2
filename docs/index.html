Bitcoin - Digital Gold


Similar to gold, there is a finite number of Bitcoin that will ever be produced (mined). New Bitcoin must also be "mined" first in order for it to enter the network.


Each time a transaction is made, it must be verified by a few people before the transaction can be added to the ledger. Miners will facilitate and verify Bitcoin transactions. As an incentive, miners are rewarded with newly generated Bitcoin for each transaction. This is called a "Block Reward".



There will only be 21 Million Bitcoins that will ever be mined


Bitcoin Mining. A Lucrative Business?

Bitcoin miners have been rewarded handsomely in terms of transaction fee revenue in the past months since the recent spike of interest. However, the difficulty of Bitcoin mining has increased consistently, which means miners must constantly upgrade their hardwares or face diminishing returns.

The Global Impact of Cryptocurrencies


Despite it's caveats, Bitcoin set to join the top 10 most popular assets worlwide as more and more people are turning to it to hedge against global crisis. It is common to see an increased Bitcoin ownership in countries where there is lack of trust in their local governments.


About 10% of the Venezualan population own cryptocurrencies as the Venezualan Bolivar was recently hit by hyperinflation induced by its dictatorial regime.
