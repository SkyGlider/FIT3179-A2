BITCOIN AROUND THE WORLD

But really, what is a Bitcoin?

Bitcoin has been the buzzword in the past months and has built itself a fanbase of veteran enthusiasts. But many are still trying to grasp the concept of the magical cryptocurrency that will seemingly "head to the moon", so lets explore how Bitcoin really works

Understanding Crypto

In essence, a cryptocurrency is a fully digital currency that works just like any other traditional currency, a financial instrument that can be used as a payment method. Cryptocurrencies are secured by cryptography which makes it impossible for counterfeiting or fraud.


The largest reason cryptocurrencies rose to its fame can be traced to its decentralized nature, cryptocurrencies are usually not issued by any central authority, therefore immune to any form of government interference.


There are more than 6000 different cryptocurrencies out there, each serving different purposes. However, Bitcoin was the pioneer in the cryptocurrency space has remains as the worlds most popular cryptocurrency since its inception in 2009.

Bitcoin - Digital Gold


Similar to gold, there is a finite number of Bitcoin that will ever be produced (mined) and not all Bitcoin is freely available in circulation yet. New Bitcoin must be "mined" first in order for it to enter the network.


Each time a transaction is made, it must be verified by a few people before the transaction can be added to the ledger. Miners will facilitate and verify Bitcoin transactions and they are rewarded with newly generated Bitcoin for each transaction verified. This is called a "Block Reward" This system incentivices more people to become "miners" to increase the rate of verification.


There is a limit of 21 Million Bitcoin that will ever be mined. Beyond that, there will be no more Block Rewards and the miners' will mianly come from transaction fees


Bitcoin Mining. A Lucrative Business?

Bitcoin miners have been rewarded handsomely in the past months since the recent spike of interest in Bitcoin. As the number transactions pile up, so has the transaction fee. However, there is a caveat. The difficulty of Bitcoin mining has increased consistently over the years, which means miners will have to constantly upgrade their hardwares or face diminishing returns.


Where The Miners At?

In the past, most of the Bitcoin miners were based in China, but amid recent news that China has made all cryptocurrency related transactions illegal, there has been a surge in cryptocurrency miners moving to the US.


Fun fact: Malaysia contributes about 5% the global Bitcoin mining capacity. Great job, Malaysians.
